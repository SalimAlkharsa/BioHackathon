PharmaAPI: Prescription CDS for prescription

What is PharmaAPI?

PharmaAPI adds an extra dimension to CDS systems by processing text notes made by the doctor such as instructions for taking prescriptions.

How PharmaAPI Helps

Current prescription CDS systems tend to not factor in doctor notes or other typical language variables relevant to prescriptions, typically requiring a secondary check by a pharmacist.

Who could use PharmaAPI

  • D2C pharmacies could use something of this concept to verify incoming prescriptions.
  • Hospitals who want to build apps on top of their EHR platforms.

How to Use the API

To use the API, follow these steps:

  1. Send a POST request to /cds endpoint.
  2. Include the patient ID in the request body as a JSON payload.
  3. Receive the CDS output in the response.
    4. Click here for the Demo

What Data is Considered in the API?

  1. Patient data (below) considered is gathered from the base Epic EHR collection.
    1. Sex
    2. Birth Date
    3. Allergy Intolerances
    4. Conditions
    5. Patient Goals
    6. Medication Statements
    7. Medication Orders
  2. Drug label data is currently not considered.
  3. Basic Flow: Patient Data is formated in a JSON and then is entered into GPT3 asking for a recomendation coupled with reasoning.

Major Disclaimer: The results of PharmaAPI are untested and are the results of an unoptimized general language model. Take any output with a grain of salt. This is a project developed during a hackathon.
